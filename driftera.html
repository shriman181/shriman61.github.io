Component Failure
Prediction Engine

Driftera's predictive models ingest real-time sensor telemetry, historical maintenance records, flight cycle data, and OEM reliability bulletins to calculate time-to-failure probability distributions for individual components.

Unlike rule-based alerts, our models reason across cross-fleet histories — meaning patterns visible in one operator's 737 fleet inform predictions for another's A320 operation.

  • Per-component failure probability scored daily
  • Anomaly detection across 500+ real-world fault scenarios
  • 6-month forward planning horizon on high-risk parts
  • Root cause linkage across interdependent subsystems
  • EHM (Engine Health Monitoring) integration out-of-box
  • Integrates with AMOS, TRAX, and SAP PM

Airworthiness
Documentation Automation

Every maintenance action requires paperwork. Logbook entries, task card sign-offs, airworthiness release certificates, FAA Form 8130-3, EASA Form 1 — the volume of compliance documentation in aviation MRO is staggering.

Driftera auto-generates compliant documentation from maintenance actions as they're logged — pulling component data, technician credentials, parts traceability, and regulatory references into structured, audit-ready records.

  • Auto-generation of work order completions and sign-offs
  • FAA, EASA, DGCA regulatory template library
  • Digital technician signatures with credential verification
  • Parts 8130-3 / Form 1 traceability chains
  • Continuous airworthiness record export for lessors
  • Audit trail immutability with timestamped versioning

Integration with supplier networks and leasing pools means recommended procurement actions are actionable in one click — not buried in a spreadsheet.

  • AOG pool prioritisation based on failure risk scores

Maintenance Bay
Scheduling Optimisation

A plane grounded in a hangar is a plane not earning revenue. Driftera's scheduling engine optimises maintenance bay utilisation by sequencing aircraft checks against bay capacity, technician certifications, tooling availability, and parts procurement windows.

The output isn't a gantt chart — it's a live, adaptive schedule that re-optimises automatically when a new AOG event or parts delay enters the picture.

  • Bay capacity planning across A/B/C/D check types
  • Technician certification and shift matching
  • Task parallelisation to minimise ground time
  • Dynamic re-sequencing on AOG or delay events
  • Integration with crew rostering systems

Market Timing

The data is ready.
The tools are ready.
Most operators aren't.

That gap is where Driftera operates.

The global commercial aircraft fleet grew from 34,000 in 2025 and is projected to hit 44,600 by 2034. Narrowbodies — the workhorses of LCC and regional carriers — run the highest utilisation rates, which means the most frequent maintenance cycles, the most documentation, the most exposure to AOG risk.

Predictive maintenance now accounts for 18% of total MRO investment — up from 11% two years ago. The institutional shift is underway, but the majority of the market still runs on reactive systems. That gap doesn't close automatically. Someone has to close it.

Airbus Skywise aggregates data from 11,000+ aircraft. United Airlines used predictive AI to prevent 300+ out-of-service events in 2024 alone. The results are unambiguous. But enterprise MRO platforms like these serve large flag carriers — not the mid-tier airlines, regional operators, and MRO third-party providers who collectively represent the majority of the market.
